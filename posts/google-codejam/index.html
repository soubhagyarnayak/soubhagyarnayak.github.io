Google Codejam

2011-05-09
5 minute read

Why I sit for Google CodeJam

Every year coders around the world compete with each other at Google CodeJam. Basically you need to come up with solutions for a set of problems. Solving the problems demand problem solving skills [:)]. Actually you need to be good at programming, algorithms and mathematics to solve the problems fast. Top Coder is another place where you can compete with the best and the brightest to solve a problem fast. For people like me, who are into jobs and into n number of other activities finding out time for top coder is not easy. But the joy of solving really interesting problems within a short amount of time and competing with other fellow geeks has its own appeal. So every year once we sit for Google Code Jam competition.

Coding at CodeJam vs Coding at Work

While coding at CodeJam, the focus is on getting the solution fast. There is no requirement to write clean or well manageable code. There is no requirement to following the best practices or writing secure code. There is no requirement to have the designs, documents, unit-test suites for the code. At the end of the day you generally throw the code. The main focus is on finding a solution. But in our professional life the need is to write code that will be used by others. The code needs to follow strict coding guidelines so that it remains manageable in future. The code will be used long after it has been coded and may be required to be maintained by different developers over a period of time. It is no surprise that in CodeJam nobody writes object oriented code, while in professional life everybody writes in OOP. The code written in professional environment needs to have the design, documentation and test-suites alongside it. In addition to it in professional environment, our code will be part of a bigger system. When code becomes part of a bigger system, we write a lot of plumbing code, code to make two things work together. We call other services, use libraries that implement the standard algorithms and expose our codes in such a way that others can consume it.

There is not much joy in writing this type of code, but at the end of the day we are paid for it. And we know that our work makes the lives of some people more comfortable. The code we write will be used by people in the years to come. In CodeJam we face really interesting problems and the process of finding the solution itself is quite gratifying. But at the end of the day we are not paid for it. And the solution will not be used by anybody; will not solve anybody’s problem.

My Experience with CodeJam

This is the 3rd time I am sitting for the CodeJam. I have never made it beyond the 2nd round. Going through the qualification is easy. You have the whole day (usually a week end) to find the solutions to 3 or 4 problems. Solving one often leads you to the next round. You can easily find 4 to 5 hours on a weekend.

In the second round, the things become a little tougher. From this round onwards it is a competition with other participants. In round 1, we will have 3 attempts 1A, 1B and 1C spread over one day so that in any time zone, one can comfortably sit at least 2 of the 3 attempts. You need to make it to top 1000 in any attempt to make it to round 2. In my experience solving one out of 3 problems is enough to make it to the next round.

In round 2 things become even tougher. The top 3000 rankers of round 1 will compete to get into 1000 slots. And the top 500 rankers used to get the Google CodeJam t-shirt. This year they have increased the number to 1000. I have failed to clear round 2 in my last 2 attempts.

Beyond round 2 is something that does not matter to me anymore. In round 3, top 1000 participants fight it out for 25 slots for the final round. Now the competition is among the top people, people who toil in top coder matches to gain speed and who are highly gifted. By the time round 3 starts I will have enjoyed a month long journey of facing CodeJam rounds on the weekends, practicing questions and going through my algorithm notes in my slack time. I will leave the activities to go for my usual work of learning and applying things. Learning new technologies and writing code that does not solve great problems, but the problems that matter. The code I write solves the problem that people face becomes a part of a huge system that makes the lives of people more comfortable and gives me a more than a decent pay check at the end of the month. The joy of solving great problems, competing with the bright coders and working under time pressure is unique. I patiently wait for the next year, the next CodeJam for that.
