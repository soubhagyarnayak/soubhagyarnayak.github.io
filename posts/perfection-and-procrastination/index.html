Perfection and Procrastination

2021-03-11
2 minute read

Procrastination is one of my weaknesses. I have seen many people having the same problem. People generally associate procrastination with laziness. But even workaholics procrastinate on many things. I associate procrastination with lack of discipline. This post is about perfection and procrastination which may sound weird. But I have often felt that a wish for perfection makes me procrastinate.

At times we aim for perfection. It is supposed to be a good thing. But I am finding the opposite. For example, I try to cover all important news and articles daily. If I come across an article which has deep content or better content, I generally keep it for later. The goal is to cover it when I have adequate time to do justice to the content. But like the untraveled road of Robert Frost, the articles remain in my backlog only. The same problem happens with a blog post which remains in perpetual draft mode and an idea which remains in backlog forever. In my case the aim for perfection is resulting in procrastination.

As a result, I am covering shallow content, leaving the good ones. I am working on low value ideas leaving the great ones. My blog is having less content. There is never a good time to do anything perfectly.

The solution to this problem is discipline. I need to do the things now as if there is no tomorrow. An article in the TODO list is of no value. But even spending some time to have a quick read can make me more informed and knowledgeable. An imperfect blog post may look bad, but it will help me to jot down my thoughts. This will refine my thinking and even nudge me to make it better later. I may not give full justice to a demanding idea. But setting the ball rolling itself will help me to explore it better.

Aiming for perfection results in procrastination. In this case, being disciplined and focusing on lesser outcome helps to get the task done.
